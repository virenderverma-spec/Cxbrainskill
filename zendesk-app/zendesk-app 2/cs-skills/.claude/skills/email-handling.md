## Email vs Chat: Key Differences

| Aspect | Chat | Email |
|--------|------|-------|
| Response time expectation | Minutes | Hours to 1 day |
| Tone | Casual, conversational | Professional but warm |
| Length | Short, back-and-forth | Complete, comprehensive |
| Formatting | Minimal | Structured with headers |
| Follow-up | Real-time | May need multiple emails |
| Context | Live session | Must be self-contained |
| Signature | Usually none | Professional signature |

## Email Response Structure

### Standard Email Format

```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject] - [Brief Status/Resolution]

Hi [Name],

[Opening - Acknowledge their email and issue]

[Body - Address their concern with solution/information]

[Next steps - What happens now or what they need to do]

[Closing - Warm sign-off]

Best,
[Agent Name]
Meow Mobile Support

---
Need quick help? Chat with Mochi in the Meow Mobile app 24/7!
```

### Email Sections Explained

**Subject Line:**
- Keep original subject with "Re:"
- Add brief status if helpful: "Re: Can't activate eSIM - Steps to resolve"

**Opening (1-2 sentences):**
- Thank them for reaching out
- Acknowledge the specific issue
- Show you understand

**Body (main content):**
- Address ALL points in their email
- Use numbered steps for instructions
- Use headers for multiple topics
- Be thorough - they can't ask immediate follow-ups

**Next Steps:**
- Clear action items
- Timeline expectations
- What you'll do vs what they need to do

**Closing:**
- Warm but professional
- Offer continued help
- Include signature

## Handling Multi-Issue Emails

When customers raise multiple issues in one email:

### Step 1: Identify All Issues

Read carefully and list every distinct issue/question:
- Issue 1: [topic]
- Issue 2: [topic]
- Issue 3: [topic]

### Step 2: Address Each Systematically

```
Subject: Re: [Main Issue] - Answers to your questions

Hi [Name],

Thanks for reaching out. You had a few questions, so let me address each one:

**Regarding [Issue 1]:**
[Complete answer]

**Regarding [Issue 2]:**
[Complete answer]

**Regarding [Issue 3]:**
[Complete answer]

Let me know if any of these need more clarification!

Best,
[Agent Name]
Meow Mobile Support
```

### Step 3: Prioritize if Urgent

If one issue is urgent (service down, payment problem), address that first:

```
First, let me address the most pressing issue - [urgent problem]:
[Solution]

Now for your other questions:
[Rest of response]
```

## Email Thread Management

### Reading Email Threads

When handling an email with history:

1. **Read bottom-up** - Start from the oldest message
2. **Note what's been tried** - Don't repeat previous agent's steps
3. **Check for promises** - Honor commitments made
4. **Identify sentiment trend** - Getting better or worse?

### Continuing a Thread

```
Hi [Name],

Following up on your case about [issue]...

[If resolved:]
I'm happy to report that [resolution].

[If in progress:]
I wanted to update you that [status]. We expect [next step] by [timeline].

[If need info:]
I'm still looking into this, but I need [info] to proceed.
```

### Closing a Thread

```
Hi [Name],

Just checking in - were you able to [action from previous email]?

If everything's working now, great! If you're still having trouble, let me know and I'll keep digging.

Best,
[Agent Name]
```

## Email Tone Guidelines

### DO:

- **Be warm but professional**: "Thanks for reaching out!" not "Hey!"
- **Be thorough**: Email readers can't ask immediate follow-ups
- **Use their name**: Personalization matters
- **Acknowledge wait time**: "Thanks for your patience" if delayed
- **Format for readability**: Use bullets, numbers, bold headers
- **Proofread**: Email typos look unprofessional
- **Sign off warmly**: "Best," "Thanks," "Take care"

### DON'T:

- **Use chat-speak**: No "u" for "you", no "thx" for "thanks"
- **Be too casual**: No "lol", "haha", or excessive emojis
- **Leave questions unanswered**: Address everything they asked
- **Make them dig for info**: Put key info at the top
- **Blame other teams**: "Our system" not "the engineering team"
- **Be robotic**: Show personality within professional bounds

### Tone Examples

**Too casual:**
> hey! so ur esim isnt working huh? no worries lets fix it lol

**Too formal:**
> Dear Valued Customer, We are in receipt of your inquiry regarding your electronic SIM card. Please be advised that...

**Just right:**
> Hi Sarah! I'm sorry to hear your eSIM isn't cooperating. Let me help you get this sorted out.

## Email-Specific Scenarios

### Scenario: Customer Forwarded a Receipt/Confirmation

Customer forwarded an automated email asking about it:

```
Hi [Name],

I see you received [type of email - receipt, confirmation, etc.]. Let me explain what this means:

[Explanation of what the email indicates]

[Any action they need to take, or confirmation that no action needed]

Does this help clarify things?
```

### Scenario: Customer CC'd Someone

When customer CC's a spouse, family member, or other party:

- Include all parties in reply (Reply All)
- Address primary contact by name
- Keep response appropriate for multiple readers

### Scenario: Customer is Angry (Email)

Angry emails require extra care:

```
Hi [Name],

I completely understand your frustration, and I'm truly sorry for this experience. This isn't how things should work, and I want to make it right.

[Address their specific complaint with empathy]

Here's what I'm doing to fix this:
[Concrete actions]

I'll personally follow up to make sure this gets resolved. You can reply directly to me at any time.

Sincerely,
[Agent Name]
```

### Scenario: No Response to Previous Email

Following up when customer hasn't replied:

```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject] - Following up

Hi [Name],

I wanted to check in on my previous email about [issue].

[If you asked for info:]
Were you able to find [info needed]? Once I have that, I can [next step].

[If you provided a solution:]
Did the steps I sent work for you? Let me know if you're still having trouble.

[If you processed something:]
Just confirming - did you receive [refund/credit/etc.]?

No rush, but I want to make sure you're taken care of!

Best,
[Agent Name]
```

## Email Response Times

| Priority | Target Response | Criteria |
|----------|-----------------|----------|
| Urgent | < 2 hours | Service down, payment issue, security |
| High | < 4 hours | Active problem affecting usage |
| Normal | < 24 hours | Questions, general support |
| Low | < 48 hours | Feedback, feature requests |

**Setting expectations:**
- If you can't resolve immediately, acknowledge receipt
- Tell them when to expect a full response
- Better to under-promise and over-deliver
